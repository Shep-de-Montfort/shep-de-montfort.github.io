National Labor Relations Board

Enforcing the NLRA

As an ILR student at Cornell, interning for the NLRB was a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity and the most practical application of everything taught in my degree. Everyone at the agency worked extremely hard and put in long hours because they really cared deeply about ensuring fair labor practices. After proving myself, I was trusted with a significant amount of responsibility. I served as the information officer, acting as the first point of contact for individuals experiencing unfair labor practices and filing the initial charge reports. It was an extremely important role because you are quite literally serving as the representative of the federal government to someone during a highly sensitive time in their life. I also conducted independent investigations, which involved taking affidavits, reviewing evidence, and collecting documents. I analyzed case precedent to write Final Investigative Reports evaluating the legal merit of the charges for the region. Additionally, I assisted the legal team with trial preparation and complex backpay calculations. The seriousness, depth of responsibility, and intellectual bandwidth of the role absolutely matches my work at BCG, except I was only 20 and 21 years old at the time. The last thing to note is the discipline to apply case law and knowing how to ask the right questions to get a full story really taught me how to think logically.
