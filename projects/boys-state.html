Boys State

Simulating the Nevada state government

I wanted control behind the scenes, so I took on the lobbyist role at Boys State, where I had a massive pool of funds to distribute, authored bills, and pulled strings to get people to do exactly what I wanted. My favorite part of the program was a negotiation exercise where groups of five had to allocate a fixed set of points (20, 10, 5, and two negative scores), requiring at least three signatures to lock in the final split. While most groups got bogged down in endless discussion, I saw the leverage immediately because I already knew one guy in my group. Right as it started, I quickly whispered to a stranger next to me, "Pretend you know me." I then addressed the whole group, announced that the three of us already knew each other and were taking the positive points, and allocated the 20 to myself, 10 to my friend, and 5 to the stranger. We signed the paper and finalized the deal before anyone else even had a chance to speak.
