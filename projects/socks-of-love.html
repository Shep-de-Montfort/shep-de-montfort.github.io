2017–2019 Entrepreneurship

Socks of Love

Venture through philanthropy

Completing community service hours was a core pillar of my high school experience. Despite this emphasis, many of my friends would scramble to find service hours just days before they were due. They would either get in trouble for missing the deadline or bypass the service entirely by purchasing donation items. I realized the best way to help them was to start a student-led service organization. To be fair to my friends, other available service opportunities were often intimidating and felt low impact. For example, a common experience involved going to an unfamiliar location, taking orders from someone three times our age, and performing a task like pouring soup into a bowl at a food drive, which was something people could realistically just do themselves. I figured it would be much more engaging and fun to lead our own initiatives.

The result was Socks of Love. We partnered with local high and middle schools to collect socks and toiletries, and we then found partner organizations to hand out the wrapped goods ourselves. This experience taught us entrepreneurship, initiative, and the true reward of service. As part of Socks of Love, I even had the opportunity to speak at City Hall on the importance of student-led community service. I am also proud that students after me continued Socks of Love!
