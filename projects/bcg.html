2023–Present Strategy & Operations

Consulting at BCG

The start of my professional life

If you want to make a generalist management consultant squirm, just ask them "what do you do." The reason it is hard for us to answer is because the answer changes every month. A much more fair question would be "what are you doing right now?" The other thing that makes it difficult is we can't share too much about who our clients are and what we work on, but I will do my best.

As a consultant, here are probably the skills I have taken away:

Becoming smarter by being forced to adapt and really think rather than relying on memorizing a process, which means using first principles to strip problems down to what is basic and true, building testable hypotheses, and learning to read complex data from pharmaceuticals to auto manufacturing in minutes while others are presenting.

Ramping up quickly on topics I know nothing about, partnering with people I have never met before, and dropping into places I have never been.

Taking in more information than I could ever imagine and instantly finding the signal for what really matters.

Knowing how and when to be eighty-twenty in my approach so I can land on a directional answer and move forward.

Managing a massive list of tasks and priorities that I know I will never actually finish.

Building real relationships with clients when we are both forced to interact and be nice to each other, breaking past that corporate barrier to develop a genuine connection.

Getting the most out of my team by showing empathy, remembering exactly what it felt like to be in their shoes, and teaching them to be the best consultants they can be.

Strategic communication and persuasion, learning to pick my words carefully because guiding stakeholders along a narrative arc where they willingly arrive at a conclusion works so much better than forcing the most direct logical path.

Self-standing narrative design, building simple and effective slide decks where the core strategy is entirely transparent through the headers alone without needing a live voiceover.

Accelerated credibility, knowing how to command authority and earn the trust of veteran executives and sixty-year-old CEOs when I am the youngest person in the room by a margin of decades.

Organizational maneuvering, learning how to read the room, decode competing incentives, and navigate the complex internal politics of massive corporate structures.

Extreme ownership under fire, stepping into the hot seat to confidently defend complex legacy work products I did not personally build when clients demand immediate accountability.

Advanced financial and operational modeling, translating complex corporate realities into dynamic Excel models for everything from standard net present value scenarios to a complete factory and product line redesign.

Early data automation and pipeline engineering, setting up complex workflows in Alteryx to clean and automate massive datasets long before generative AI tools made it mainstream.

Resource and leverage optimization, mobilizing external experts and coordinating teams to dramatically accelerate the pace of delivery.

Radical adaptability, becoming completely immune to environmental friction by learning to execute high-level work from anywhere, whether that means a plane, a car, the sidewalk, or a hotel closet.

Concise business writing, mastering the art of high-impact, ultra-brief email communication that respects the reader's time and drives immediate action.
