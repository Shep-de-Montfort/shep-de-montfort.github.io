High School Cross Country

Running is so hard

I was ninety-five pounds wet my freshman year, and I finished high school at maybe one hundred and thirty pounds. I was not going to be good at any sport other than cross country. I trained for it even in the off season with Coach Pendola, so I was running all year round. It was incredibly difficult. You would finish school at three in the afternoon, run in zero-degree weather until six in the evening, and then have to go home to study for a test the next day. I was a terrible runner and never placed well. However, given my leadership qualities and consistency, I was still named captain my senior year despite my lack of speed. Running taught me a lot of things about health and wellness that other people would not catch on to until ten years later, such as ice baths and rolling out. It also taught me that life can always get tougher, and sitting at a desk all day is never as bad as running in ninety-degree weather up a mountain with a sore throat and an injured leg at 7 AM on a Saturday.
