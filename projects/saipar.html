South African Institute of Policy and Research

Researching the participatory mechanisms of NGOs

Cornell has an amazing program where students partner with the South African Institute of Policy and Research to write research papers around topics local NGOs are interested in. We partnered with SAIPAR and the Zambian Governance Foundation (ZGF) to research how effectively NGOs involve local communities in their projects and how NGOs can best involve communities from planning to feedback. We conducted interviews with NGOs and local community leaders to evaluate how effective 6 different NGOs' participatory practices were. Through our case studies and interviews, we found that while Zambian NGOs frequently consult local communities, they rarely cede actual decision-making power or financial control, leaving most projects trapped in a pattern of top-down tokenism. To fix this, we recommend that NGOs decentralize their budgets to local management committees, deploy two-way communication channels via accessible mobile technology, and adopt community-led evaluation methods to shift true ownership back to the people. Our paper was recently cited in a real academic journal, the Interdisciplinary Journal of Rural & Community Studies. That officially makes me a cited researcher, which is super cool!
